# Product

## Register

product

## Users

South African job seekers, primarily recent graduates and mid-career professionals navigating a competitive job market. They apply to government jobs (Z83 forms, DPSA listings), private sector roles (Indeed, PNet, LinkedIn), and use the platform daily to track applications and tailor CVs. Many are applying to 10+ jobs simultaneously and need the process automated. Primary device is mobile but they work on desktop for document-heavy tasks.

## Product Purpose

CareerGate automates the job application pipeline: scraping listings, tailoring CVs with AI to mirror ATS requirements, filling government forms (Z83), sending applications via email, and tracking everything in a dashboard. Success means a user applies to more jobs with less effort and gets more interview callbacks.

## Brand Personality

Empowering, practical, confident. The brand speaks plainly — no corporate fluff. It feels like a smart tool built by someone who understands the South African job market firsthand. Tagline: "Your path to being employed."

## Anti-references

- Not LinkedIn (too social, too corporate, too blue)
- Not generic SaaS (cream + indigo, Stripe-clone aesthetics, hero metrics with gradient accents)
- Not government portal (grey, bureaucratic, inaccessible)
- Not recruitment agency (stock photos of handshakes, hollow motivational copy)

## Design Principles

1. **The tool does the work** — every screen should reduce effort, not add steps. If a user has to fill in something manually that we could infer, that is a failure.
2. **Density earns trust** — job seekers want information, not whitespace. Show more, hide less. Tables over cards where data density matters.
3. **South African context** — Z83 forms, DPSA listings, local company names, rands. The product should feel built for this market, not adapted from somewhere else.
4. **Orange means action** — the brand orange (#F7941D) is reserved for primary actions and progress states. Not decoration.
5. **Clarity over cleverness** — labels, button copy, and status names should be immediately obvious to a first-time user.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Strong color contrast given orange on white usage. Touch targets minimum 44px for mobile users. Support for users with limited data — no heavy animations or large unoptimised assets.
